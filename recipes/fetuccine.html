Fetuccine Alfredo

Description

Prepare to fall in love with the ultimate comfort food: a creamy, dreamy Fetuccine Alfredo. This isn't just pasta; it's a luscious embrace of tender fettuccine swathed in a rich, velvety sauce crafted from butter, heavy cream, and freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Each twirl of the fork promises a symphony of decadent flavors, a testament to simple ingredients coming together to create something truly extraordinary. Get ready to transform your kitchen into an Italian trattoria and your dinner table into a haven of pure, unadulterated deliciousness.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz (about half a standard box) fettuccine pasta
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese (Parmigiano-Reggiano, if possible), plus extra for serving
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste (optional, but highly recommended)

Steps

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add the fettuccine and cook according to package directions until al dente (firm to the bite). Reserve about 1 cup of the starchy pasta water before draining the pasta. This pasta water is your secret weapon for a silky sauce!
  2. Melt the Butter: While the pasta is cooking, in a large skillet or wide pan over medium-low heat, melt the cubed butter. Swirl the pan to coat the bottom.
  3. Add the Cream: Pour in the heavy cream and gently warm it through, stirring occasionally. Do not let it boil. You want it to be hot but not simmering vigorously.
  4. Combine & Emulsify: Once the pasta is drained, immediately add the hot fettuccine directly to the skillet with the butter and cream. Using tongs, gently toss the pasta to coat it thoroughly.
  5. Stir in Parmesan: Gradually add the freshly grated Parmesan cheese, a little at a time, continuously tossing the pasta. The cheese will melt into the warm sauce, creating a thick, glossy coating.
  6. Adjust Consistency (with Pasta Water): If the sauce seems too thick or sticky, add a tablespoon or two of the reserved pasta water at a time, tossing constantly, until you reach your desired creamy consistency. The starch in the water helps to emulsify the sauce.
  7. Season & Serve: Season with salt to taste (remember Parmesan is salty, so taste first!). Add a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper if desired. Serve immediately, garnished with extra grated Parmesan cheese.
